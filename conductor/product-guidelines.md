# Product Guidelines: Shared AI Storytelling RPG

## 1. Design & UX Principles
- **Immersion First**: The user interface should be clean, focused on typography and storytelling. Avoid excessive UI clutter.
- **Interactive Feedback**: Every action taken by a player must have a visible impact on the world state.
- **Tone Consistency**: The AI-generated narrative must align with the established world era, environment, and tone (e.g., medieval, adventure).

## 2. AI Interaction Guidelines
- **Narrative Pacing**: Story sessions should be structured with a clear beginning, middle, and resolution.
- **Structured Outputs**: All AI-driven state updates must be validated as valid JSON before being committed to the master world state.
- **Guardrails**: The "World Bible" should be used to prevent lore contradictions and ensure world coherence.

## 3. Asynchronous Multiplayer Standards
- **Wait-Less Play**: Players should be able to start their story sessions independently of other players.
- **Cross-Session Connectivity**: The "While You Were Away" summaries must highlight direct or indirect consequences caused by other players.
- **Shared World Consequence**: A world event caused by one player should be discoverable or impactful for others in that same region.

## 4. Visual Identity
- **AI Portraits**: Character portraits should be stylistically consistent with the world's era and tone.
- **Minimalist Aesthetic**: Use cards and typography-driven designs for story scenes and choices.
